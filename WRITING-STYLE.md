# Writing Style Reference — analogattention

Use this as a guide when writing journal entries. All content should sound like it comes from one voice.

---

## Voice

- First person. Always "I", "me", "my". This is a personal journal.
- Conversational but intelligent. Like talking to someone you respect at a coffee shop.
- Honest about uncertainty. "I didn't expect", "I'm still figuring that out."
- Dry humour, never forced. Observations that happen to be funny, never trying to be. ("Funnily enough, this accounts for 80%+ of data breaches worldwide.")

## Sentence Style

- Short paragraphs. 1–3 sentences. Let the thought breathe.
- Semicolons to join related thoughts instead of starting new sentences.
- Single punchy sentences stand alone as paragraphs. ("Remove the chance of distraction.")
- Starts sentences with conjunctions and fragments when natural. ("Except, the issue is...")
- Mixes contractions ("didn't", "you'll") with full forms ("I did not expect") — full forms used for emphasis.

## Structure

- Section headers are declarative and short. "The Trigger", "Sit Still", "Why Does This Matter?"
- Horizontal rules (---) between major sections.
- End sections with weight. The last line should land, not trail off.
- No filler transitions. No "In this article, we'll discuss..."
- Bullet points only for actionable lists, never for prose.

## Content Approach

- Specific numbers and real examples. 13.4 years, 171 notifications, 7 days. Never vague when you can be exact.
- Personal anecdotes before general advice. Show what happened, then let the reader draw conclusions.
- Professional/technical references used naturally. SaaS terminology, PICNIC problems, algorithms — used to illuminate, not to impress.
- Direct address to the reader. "Do this right this second." Pull them into the action.
- No motivational fluff. No "you've got this." Present the reality and trust the reader.

## Formatting

- **Bold** for key phrases and emphasis, sparingly.
- *Italics* for internal thoughts or subtle stress.
- Exclamation marks almost never used.
- No emoji in body text.

## Things To Avoid

- Over-explaining. Trust the reader.
- Wrapping up with a neat bow. Leave questions open if they're genuinely open.
- The word "journey" more than once per entry.
- Passive voice when active is available.
- Hedging language. Say what you mean.
